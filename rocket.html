Rocket Design and Gallery

Nose Shape: Bullet shape for aerodynamic efficiency, reducing air resistance, enhancing stability, and minimizing drag for higher altitudes and speeds with less energy.

Fins: Rectangular fins for simplicity and effectiveness in providing stability, counteracting wind disturbances.

Base: Rectangular base for a stable launch surface and controlled takeoff.

Fuselage Structure: Lattices and frames for longitudinal rigidity, maintaining shape, and supporting transverse loads, creating a lightweight yet robust structure.

Recovery System: Equipped with a parachute, buzzer for localization, and GPS to ensure safe and controlled descent, protecting both the payload and rocket structure.

Vertical Flight Simulation

This graph illustrates the behavior of the rocket during its vertical flight. The left axis represents altitude and vertical acceleration, while the right axis shows vertical velocity. Several key events are highlighted:
1. Motor Ignition: The motor ignites at the start of the flight, providing the necessary thrust.
2. Motor Burnout: The motor completes its burn in approximately 4 seconds.
3. Apogee: The highest point reached by the rocket before beginning its descent.
4. Recovery System Deployment: The recovery system is activated to ensure a safe landing.
This simulation is crucial for understanding the rocket’s performance and ensuring the flight’s safety and effectiveness.
